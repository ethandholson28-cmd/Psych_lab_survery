Cognitive Rhythm Study

Mapping the weekly cognitive cycle of college students
Welcome & Consent
STEP 01 / 08

This study investigates whether Dartmouth students exhibit a predictable weekly cognitive rhythm, and how social behavior (going out) influences cognitive readiness across the week.

You will complete four short cognitive tasks after answering a few quick questions. The full session takes about 2–3 minutes.

Tasks include:

① Word memorization (10 words, 20s study)  ·  ② Mental counting (25-second internal timer)  ·  ③ Reaction time (5 trials, averaged)  ·  ④ Stroop task (executive function)  ·  ⑤ Word recall

All data is anonymous. By continuing, you consent to participate.
